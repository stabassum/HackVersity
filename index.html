FAQ


  1. What is a Hackathon?

    Typically a 24 hour long event where you work on a team to develop an idea and create a working prototype from scratch.
    Hacking in this connotation means creative problem solving. It’s a fun and welcoming atmosphere with opportunities.You don’t
    have to be a programmer and you certainly don’t have to be majoring in Computer Science.

  2. What if I don't have a team?

    That’s alright to come in without a team, you will have a chance to meet fellow hackers and form teams at the event.

  3. What are the rules?

    Refer to Major League Hacking’s Code of Conduct

  4. What should I bring?

    A laptop and charger, you could also bring your sleeping bag and a change of clothes to make yourself comfortable.

  5. What if I don't know how to code?

    It’s really up to you! A website, an app, a game, robotics, anything you can think of really.

  6. How big should the teams be?

    The recommended team size is 4 people.

  7. Will I get travel reimbursement?

    We are working on it, however we cannot guarantee to provide you with travel reimbursement.

  8. Who can attend?

    This is open to any students in the US. You don’t have to be a computer science student, you don’t even have to know how to code.

  9. How much does it cost?

    Absolutely nothing to the hacker. We got you covered.

  10. Who owns my project?

    You and your team do! We, and the sponsors don’t claim any rights to your project. You are free to do whatever you like with it.

  11. Can I start before I arrive?

    No, while you can start thinking of ideas beforehand, you can not write a single line of code before hacking officially begins on the day of the event.
